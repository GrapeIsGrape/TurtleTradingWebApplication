Strategy documentation & reference

This web application implements the Turtle Trading strategy and related analytics. It covers both System 1 and System 2 variations with systematic entry, exit, and position sizing rules.
System 1 — 20-Day Breakout

Long Position

ENTRY
  • Price breaks above 20-day high
  • No existing position in ticker
  • Skip next signal after a winning trade
EXIT
  • Price closes below 10-day low
  • Stop loss: Entry − (2 × ATR-20)

Short Position

ENTRY
  • Price breaks below 20-day low
  • Skip next signal after a winning trade
EXIT
  • Price closes above 10-day high
  • Stop loss: Entry + (2 × ATR-20)

Best for quick trends; avoids whipsaw with Skip Winner logic.

System 2 — 55-Day Breakout

Long Position

ENTRY
  • Price breaks above 55-day high
EXIT
  • Price closes below 20-day low
  • Stop loss: Entry − (2 × ATR-20)

Short Position

ENTRY
  • Price breaks below 55-day low
EXIT
  • Price closes above 20-day high
  • Stop loss: Entry + (2 × ATR-20)

Best for major trends; fewer false signals, higher-quality entries.

Key Indicators
Average True Range (ATR) — Volatility measurement. High ATR = bigger moves = smaller position. Used for stops & sizing.
N-Day High/Low — Highest/lowest price over N days. Defines breakout levels.
Moving Averages (5/10/20/30/50/100/200) — Confirm trend. Bullish when shorter MAs are above longer MAs.
Position Sizing & Risk
Unit Size: Shares = Risk per trade ÷ (2 × ATR). E.g., $100 ÷ ($5 ATR × 2) = 10 shares.
Stop Distance: 2 × ATR below entry (defines max loss per trade).
Max Risk/Trade: 1–2% of account. Typical Turtle rule: 2%.
Sector Limit: Max 1–2 units per GICS sector to avoid concentration.
Multiple Signals — Priority Ranking

When multiple breakouts fire on the same day, use this ranking:

1) Sector Filter First

Already have an open trade in that GICS sector? Skip the new setup.

2) Volatility Rank (Highest First)

Choose the ticker with the biggest 20-day ATR.

3) Momentum Strength

Prefer the one trading farther above its 200-day MA, or with a wider breakout margin.

4) Liquidity Tie-Breaker

Higher average daily dollar volume = smoother fills and tighter spreads.

5) Still Tied?

Take the first symbol in scan order (FIFO) or randomize. Don't overthink it.
