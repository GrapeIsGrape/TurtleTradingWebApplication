Based on last 252 trading days of daily returns — Turtle & DCA equity positions only. Options and bonds are excluded (derivatives / no daily price series).

Correlation measures how similarly two positions move day-to-day, from −1 (perfectly opposite) to +1 (perfectly in sync).

● High (> 0.8) — Move almost in lockstep. Holding both provides little diversification; a single bad trade hits both positions.
● Moderate (0.3 – 0.8) — Partially correlated. Some diversification benefit, but still influenced by the same market forces.
● Low (−0.3 – 0.3) — Move largely independently. Good diversification; losses in one are unlikely to coincide with losses in the other.
● Negative (< −0.3) — Tend to move in opposite directions. Acts as a natural hedge; one position may offset losses in the other.
